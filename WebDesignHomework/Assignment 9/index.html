How to Clean Dirty Sneakers

Sneakers are an investment in style, comfort, and durability, but they can get dirty very easily. Keeping them clean is important for maintaining their longevity and overall appearance.

How to Wash Your Shoes in a Washing Machine

Washing your sneakers in a washing machine can be a faster and more convenient method, but it is more abrasive and can cause wear on the material. It has the potential to ruin your shoes, especially if they are delicate or made from materials like suede or leather.

Materials

  • Soft brush or cloth
  • Dish soap or sneaker cleaner
  • Baking soda (for odor removal)
  • Water (warm)
  • Microfiber cloth or paper towels

Instructions

Step 1

Brush off Loose Dirt

Before cleaning, use a brush to remove any dirt, dust, or debris from the sneakers. This will help avoid rubbing dirt deeper into the material during cleaning.

Step 2

Prepare the Cleaning Solution

Mix warm water with a small amount of dish soap or sneaker cleaner. Stir to create a gentle cleaning solution.

Step 3

Clean the Sneakers

Dip your brush or cloth into the solution and gently scrub your sneakers, focusing on dirty or stained areas. Be cautious with delicate materials.

Step 4

Wash (Optional Machine Method)

If you choose to use the washing machine, place your sneakers in a laundry bag and wash on a gentle cycle with cold water.

Step 5

Dry the Sneakers

After washing, stuff your sneakers with paper towels to maintain their shape and let them air dry. Avoid putting them in the dryer to prevent damage.
